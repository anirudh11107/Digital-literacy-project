# Stay Safe Online: A Checklist for College Students

To protect your digital identity and academic data, follow these 8 actionable prevention tips:

## General Security
1. **Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Always activate MFA (using apps like Google Authenticator or SMS) on your University portals, Gmail, and social media accounts to prevent unauthorized access.
2. **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Avoid using your name, birthdate, or "123456." Use a passphrase with a mix of symbols and numbers, and never reuse the same password across different sites.
3. **Verify Official Communication:** If you receive an email or WhatsApp message about a "Scholarship" or "Exam Fee" that requires clicking a suspicious link, verify it through your college's official notice board or office first.
4. **Secure Your Devices:** Ensure your laptop and smartphone have the latest OS updates and a reputable antivirus/anti-malware suite installed to patch security vulnerabilities.
5. **Be Cautious on Public Wi-Fi:** Avoid logging into banking or student portals while connected to free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. Use a VPN if you must access sensitive data on a public network.
6. **Manage Social Media Privacy:** Limit the amount of personal information (like your phone number or home address) visible to "Public" or "Friends of Friends" to prevent doxxing or targeted phishing.

## Financial & UPI Safety
7. **The "PIN is for Paying" Rule:** Remember that you **NEVER** need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to *receive* money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "claim a prize" or "receive a refund," it is a scam.
8. **Verify UPI IDs and Names:** Before hitting 'Send' on any UPI app (BHIM, Google Pay, PhonePe), double-check the verified name of the recipient. Scammers often use names that look like official entities but have slight spelling variations.

## How to Report Cybercrime in India
If you or someone you know falls victim to a cyberattack, report it immediately to the authorities:

* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [https://www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Available 24/7 for financial fraud reporting)
